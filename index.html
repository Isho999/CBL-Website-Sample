What is J.L.?

J.L. Ilsley is a public high school located in Halifax, Nova Scotia, Canada. It is available for students in grades 9-12. As of 2021, a new school was built, with the old school being prepared for demolition and to be replaced with a football field. Before the new school was built, the previous school had been home to students for more than 50 years. The motto for J.L. Ilsley is "Justitia et Doctrina per Tempus" - ‘Justice and Wisdom Through Time’

Who was James Lorimer Ilsley?

James Lorimer Ilsley (January 3rd, 1894-January 14th, 1967) was a Canadian politician and jurist. Born in Somerset, Nova Scotia, Ilsley attended Acadia University and Dalhousie University. He was admitted to the bar in 1916 to practice law. Ilsley continued to study law in Yarmouth and Halifax, Nova Scotia. He was elected to the House of Commons, representing the Liberal party, in the 1926 election. Ilsley retained his position during the 1930 election, when the Liberals were in opposition.

Ilsley’s experience within the government influenced Prime Minister William Lyon Mackenzie King to bring Ilsley into Cabinet as Minister of National Revenue. By 1940, Ilsley had gained so much reputation that he was promoted to Minister of Finance. He maintained this role for the duration of World War 2, and was responsible for managing funds to account for the damage caused by the war.
